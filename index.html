Exploring the Frontiers of Quantum Science

We are a theoretical and experimental research group at the Department of Physics, Guru Nanak Dev University, Amritsar.

Welcome to the Quantum Optics & Quantum Information Lab

Our research group, led by Dr. Harpreet Singh, is dedicated to exploring the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics and their applications in next-generation technologies. We focus on areas such as quantum entanglement, quantum computing, secure communication, and precision measurements.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines rigorous theoretical modeling with cutting-edge experimental techniques. We aim to tackle some of the most profound questions in modern physics and contribute to the development of quantum technologies that will shape the future.

Our laboratory is equipped with state-of-the-art instrumentation for quantum optics research. We collaborate extensively with national and international researchers to foster a vibrant and comprehensive scientific environment. Whether you're a prospective student, a research partner, or simply curious about our work, we invite you to explore our website.

Our Research

Quantum Entanglement & Bell's Inequalities

We investigate the non-local properties of quantum mechanics through the study of entangled photon pairs. Our experiments aim to test the foundations of quantum theory against local realistic models by violating Bell's inequalities with high statistical significance. This research is crucial for understanding the nature of reality and for developing protocols in quantum information processing.

Quantum Cryptography & Secure Communication

Harnessing the principles of quantum mechanics, we are developing and implementing quantum key distribution (QKD) protocols. Our goal is to create unconditionally secure communication channels, immune to eavesdropping attacks. We are working on both fiber-based and free-space QKD systems for practical applications.

Theoretical & Computational Quantum Physics

Our theoretical work focuses on modeling complex quantum systems and designing novel quantum algorithms. Using advanced computational techniques, we simulate the behavior of quantum materials and explore the dynamics of quantum information. This synergy between theory and computation guides our experimental investigations and opens new avenues for discovery.
